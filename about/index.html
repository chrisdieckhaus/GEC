About GEC

Female underrepresentation in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math fields continues to be a major source of concern in the United States and across the globe. From a young age, girls are pushed towards traditional female roles and activities, while being told that science and math is "for boys." Universities filled with male-dominated science and math classrooms fail to offer sufficient support structures to inspire females to pursue an engineering major. By the time college students are ready to graduate and enter the workforce, only a quarter of the engineering field is female, with fields such as petroleum engineering as low as nine percent. Intimidation, gender norms, and other social pressures against girls who show an interest in science-related topics steer most females away from STEM fields and into traditional fields "for women."

It is this perception of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math fields as a "manâ€™s job" that Girls Engineering Change strives to correct. Through early exposure and sustained mentorship, Girls Engineering Change hopes to provide a platform to get females interested in engineering and keep them interested through continuing sessions, mentorship, and leadership.

With challenges such as climate change, global health, and energy sustainability, engineers are in high demand in the United States and around the world. Currently, the United States faces a shortage of engineers to address these pressing issues. Girls Engineering Change hopes to eliminate this shortage by tapping into the female population and inspiring a new generation of engineers - an equitable and inspired generation. Girls Engineering Change seeks to inspire children at a young age to connect to the pressing issues of our generation and challenge them to make a difference in one session and throughout their lives.
